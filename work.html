Formula Student Automotive Engineering

Timeline: 2019-2022 (PS: I am sitting inside the cockpit!)

  • Vehicle Dynamics Engineer (2019-2021)
  • Department Head of Steering and Suspension (2021-2022)

Suspension System

Design Developed: Designed a double wishbone suspension with direct actuation at the front and pushrod actuation at the rear, targeting optimal motion ratio, camber gain, roll center height, and low unsprung mass. Materials were selected using Ashby plots, buckling calculations, and validated through ANSYS FEA.

Problem Solved: Packaging constraints from the hybrid powertrain made symmetric suspension layouts infeasible at the rear while maintaining desired kinematic behavior.

Impact:
  • Achieved target dynamic camber and roll center control under bump and rebound.
  • Reduced unsprung mass while maintaining structural safety.
  • Validated suspension kinematics using Lotus Shark simulations and ensured no REIB (Rod End in Bending).

Skills Developed: Suspension kinematics, FEA, materials selection, Lotus Shark, load path design, packaging-driven tradeoffs

Steering System

Design Developed: Designed a rack-and-pinion steering system based on Ackermann geometry, optimized using Lotus Shark software to achieve low-speed agility, minimal bump steer, and predictable driver feedback. The system was tailored for tight autocross conditions with a 4:1 rack ratio, 45° maximum inner wheel steer angle, and a compact rack packaging aligned with front suspension hardpoints.

Problem Solved: The primary challenge was achieving high maneuverability at low speeds (tight corners, hairpins) while avoiding excessive bump steer and steering effort, all under packaging constraints imposed by suspension geometry and front bulkhead structure. I also developed a comprehensive load calculation spreadsheet which helped in the design methodology and analysis of the lightweight mounts.

Impact:
  • Reduced front bump steer to ≤0.01° at ±40 mm bump/rebound, and rear to ≤0.04°, through rack position and tie-rod length optimization.
  • Achieved progressive Ackermann behavior, peaking dynamically at ~216%, improving low-speed cornering response.
  • Enabled a tight minimum turning radius (~2.2 m relative to CG), significantly improving autocross performance.
  • Reduced overall steering system weight by 30%.
  • Oversaw the design and manufacturing of a novel FRP steering wheel which contributed to us winning 1st place in innovation at Formula Imperial (New Delhi, India).

Skills Developed: Steering kinematics, Ackermann geometry, bump steer optimization, Lotus Shark simulation, hardpoint optimization, vehicle dynamics, driver-centric design, packaging tradeoff analysis

Safety, Braking and Ergonomics

Safety and reliability: Worked very closely with data acquisition (PCB) and powertrain engineers to validate calculations, implement sensor positioning and performed ergonomics analysis.

Impact:
  • Performed brake thermal analysis to ensure safe operation under high-speed conditions and prevent overheating.
  • Performed ergonomic analysis in CATIA to ensure driver safety and control.
  • Validated sensor placement and data acquisition systems for real-time monitoring.

Skills Developed: Thermal FEA, Ergonomics, PCB, Data Analysis, Safety Design.

SUMMARY- FS Engineering Experience

Working in Formula Student inculcated a sense of discipline and innovative thinking ability. Beyond subsystem design, I learned how decisions made on design softwares translate directly to manufacturability, reliability, and first principles thinking. I was involved in hands-on manufacturing of mounts and structural components, learning how tolerances, CNC machining, welding and machining principles affect whether a design succeeds or fails in practice.

Equally important was the team aspect. I had the opportunity to lead sub-teams, create build schedules, coordinate across disciplines, and drive technical discussions around tradeoffs, risk, and performance metrics. Under tight timelines and limited resources, I learned how to prioritize, communicate clearly, and make pragmatic engineering decisions while keeping the team aligned and motivated.

Competing and winning awards reinforced the value of process, preparation, and teamwork over individual brilliance. Formula Student gave me early exposure to manufacturing engineering, mechanical design, NPI-style iteration, and technical leadership, business class presentations, etc and it shaped how I approach complex engineering problems today.
